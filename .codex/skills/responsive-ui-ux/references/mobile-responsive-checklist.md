# Mobile Responsive Checklist

## Typography

- Verify headings stay readable on small screens.
- Verify body text does not feel too small, too wide, or too dense.
- Verify long labels wrap cleanly instead of overflowing.
- Verify line-height supports scanning on mobile.

## Spacing

- Verify section spacing is consistent from top to bottom.
- Verify card padding does not waste too much mobile space.
- Verify grouped controls have enough separation for touch.
- Verify stacked elements still feel intentional, not collapsed.

## Images and Logos

- Verify faces remain visible and understandable.
- Verify logos are not clipped, distorted, or too small.
- Verify screenshots preserve the important area.
- Verify `object-cover` is used only when cropping is acceptable.
- Verify `object-contain` has enough height to remain useful.

## Controls and Navigation

- Verify buttons are easy to tap.
- Verify arrow controls are visible and not cramped.
- Verify toggles, tabs, and selectors show the active state clearly.
- Verify control groups do not cause horizontal overflow unless intentionally scrollable.

## Cards, Grids, and Sections

- Verify multi-column layouts stack at the right breakpoint.
- Verify text and media order still makes sense when stacked.
- Verify card heights are not fighting variable content.
- Verify empty space is balanced, not awkward.

## Accessibility and Touch

- Verify focus states remain visible.
- Verify important actions do not rely only on hover.
- Verify contrast remains readable across themes.
- Verify tap targets are large enough for touch devices.

## Device Passes

- Mobile: verify first-load clarity, text scale, touch comfort, and media visibility.
- Tablet: verify transitions between stacked and split layouts.
- Desktop: verify the layout still feels intentional, not stretched or sparse.
